# FormulaLab, Cargolane Sales Engagement · Notes

## What this example demonstrates

- The full **11-slide sales spine** from `deck-sales`, applied in the **named-customer-and-prospect format** that B2B post-discovery decks use (your logo + their logo on the cover, deal context anchored to a real meeting date).
- The **Quiet Confident** style preset (sage green + warm slate, Source Serif Pro display) used for an enterprise dev-tools sale. This is the "established, considered" preset, deliberately picked to read as mature on a $128K ARR sale.
- A **real ROI calculation** on Slide 8, with transparent inputs (8 engineers, $220K loaded cost, 96 hours saved per integration, 35 integrations planned) and the conservative math walked out: $1.04M recovered, $128K license, 6-week payback. No unsourced "saves you 30%" claims.
- A **Next Steps slide** with named human (Sara Lin), specific date (Thursday April 25, 14:00 PT), explicit ask ("confirm by EOD Wednesday April 9"), and the mutual prep checklist for both sides. No generic "we will follow up."
- **Named references** on the Proof slide (Freightnest, Paywall, Roadrunner Travel) with company size, vertical, named contact for the reference call, and quantified outcomes. No "leading financial services firm" placeholders.
- **Honest, transparent pricing** on Slide 10 with two real tiers (Team $128K, Enterprise $210K), exclusions called out, and "when this fits / when to skip" guidance.
