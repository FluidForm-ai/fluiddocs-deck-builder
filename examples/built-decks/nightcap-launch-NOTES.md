# Nightcap, Launch Deck · Notes

## What this example demonstrates

- The full **12-slide launch spine** from `deck-launch`, applied to a **consumer product** (iOS app) instead of the B2B SaaS angle the canonical `launch-default` template shows.
- The **Studio Bold** style preset (cobalt blue + warm cream + saffron, Playfair Display serif headlines) used at full editorial weight. This is the premium-consumer preset, picked deliberately to make a tiny indie journaling app feel deliberate and curated rather than scrappy.
- A **Slide 5 demo slot** built as a static iOS phone-frame screenshot, showing the actual product flow (date, greeting, prompt, mood pills, entry, footer). Includes three quantified "look inside" stats next to the phone (68% day-30 retention, 94s median entry time, 4.9 TestFlight rating), with the "Powered by FluidDocs" attribution mark anchored bottom-right.
- A **co-located Availability + Pricing** slide (Slide 7), with three real tiers (Free, Plus $3.99/mo, Couples $5.99/mo) and a launch-week offer ribbon. No "coming soon" vagueness, no hidden pricing.
- A **strong CTA slide** (Slide 12) with a resolvable URL (nightcap.app), a real-looking QR code rendered as inline SVG, two CTAs (Download / Press kit), and three audience-specific routes (reviewers, creators, therapists).
- A **roadmap with named release dates** (Q2 shipped, Q3 September, Q4 December, Q1 2027 February), plus a "what we will not ship" section to defend the product's restraint.

## Type pack + style preset

- Pack: `skills/deck-launch/` (12-slide spine, demo at slide 5, mandatory CTA at slide 12)
- Preset: **Studio Bold**, from `skills/deck-builder/references/style-presets.md` (Preset 3)
- Typography scale declared in CSS: `11px, 14px, 18px, 22px, 34px, 60px, 112px`
