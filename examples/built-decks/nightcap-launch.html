03 of 12 · Introducing Nightcap
The product

One question. One screen. Ninety seconds.

Nightcap is the journaling app that asks one good question each evening, written by a human, tuned to your week.
One prompt, never twelve. Curated by the editorial team that built The School of Life's Conversation Cards.
Closed by default. Entries are end-to-end encrypted. The app cannot read your answers, even on the server. No streaks. No social.
A year-end read. On December 31, Nightcap stitches your year into a private 12-minute audio piece. Hearing your year back is the loop the category has been missing.
Offline-first. Works on a plane. Works at the cabin. The phone is the database.

04 of 12 · How It Works
Four moves, four nights

Open the app, answer one question, close the app, repeat.

1

The reminder lands.

A single notification, between 9pm and 10:30pm local. No badge count. No "you broke your streak."

Once a night
2

One question appears.

Written by Nightcap's editorial team, tuned to the day of the week and the season. New prompts every Sunday.

Curated, not generated
3

You write 30 seconds.

Or 3 minutes. Or you press skip. The app does not pressure. The cursor sits, the keyboard waits.

90 seconds median
4

It is gone, but kept.

The screen closes. Your answer is encrypted on the device. On December 31, it returns as an audio story only you can hear.

Read back, year end

What we will not ship
No social feed. No "share to a friend" surface. No AI-generated prompts. The category is full of those, and they pull the product in the wrong direction.
What we are protecting
90-second flow. Local-first storage. One prompt a night, written by a person. These three constraints anchor every roadmap call.
Where customer requests go
A monthly council of 8 paying users reviews the most requested features. Two of the four releases above came directly from that group.
