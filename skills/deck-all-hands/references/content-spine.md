# Content Spine, All-Hands Deck (15 slides)

All-hands decks are designed for company-wide team meetings. They balance celebration with honest reflection, communicate company health transparently, and set up the next chapter. The pace is measured. Assume the live meeting (not the deck) will carry the detailed Q&A.

---

## Slide 1, Cover / Hello

**Purpose:** Open the meeting with warmth and clarity about what's coming.

**Required elements:**
- Company logo (or variant for this moment)
- Meeting title (e.g., "Q1 All-Hands", "May 2024 Team Meeting", "Company Check-in")
- Date or month/quarter
- Optional: a visual anchor (team photo, flagship product, moment from the month)

**Failure modes:**
- Cold, corporate cover with no humanity
- Overly complex or unclear title
- Logo placed awkwardly or at wrong scale

---

## Slide 2, Agenda

**Purpose:** Set reader expectations. Show the shape of the meeting and invite them into it.

**Required elements:**
- 5-8 topic bullets (mirror the subsequent slides)
- One-liner framing (e.g., "Here's what we'll cover today:")
- Optional: time markers or mood icons if they help tone

**Failure modes:**
- Agenda so long it feels overwhelming
- Topics listed but not in order of the deck
- Generic "updates, Q&A, etc." with no personality

---

## Slide 3, Big Wins This Month

**Purpose:** Lead with momentum. Celebrate the team's concrete wins in a short, punchy format.

**Required elements:**
- 3-5 wins (not generic "good work", specific ships, milestones, or customer moments)
- One-liner per win (e.g., "Shipped self-serve dashboard", "Hit 50K paying customers", "Closed Acme partnership")
- Optional: KPI callouts or trend indicators

**Failure modes:**
- Vague wins ("great job everyone")
- Too many wins (dilutes impact)
- Wins that only matter to one team (not company-wide)

---

## Slide 4, Product Highlights

**Purpose:** Show the team what shipped last month and what's in motion.

**Required elements:**
- 2-4 product updates or feature releases
- One-liner per update explaining what it does for users
- Optional: before/after visual, customer quote, or usage metric

**Failure modes:**
- Shipping updates with no customer context
- Highly technical details (save for eng-only channels)
- Celebrating work in progress instead of shipped

---

## Slide 5, Customer Stories

**Purpose:** Make the customer real and remind the team why the work matters.

**Required elements:**
- 1-2 customer stories (short narrative, quote, or quantified result)
- Customer name and brief context (company size, use case, or vertical)
- The "so what", what changed for them, why it mattered
- Optional: customer logo or photo

**Failure modes:**
- Abstract wins without customer voice
- Stories so long they derail the meeting
- NDA violations or over-sharing of customer data

---

## Slide 6, Financial Update (Appropriate Level)

**Purpose:** Show company health transparently, but only what's appropriate for the full company, not board-level detail.

**Required elements:**
- 1-2 company-wide KPIs (ARR, MRR, headcount, burn rate, runway)
- Month-over-month or quarter-over-quarter trend (up/down/flat with % or $)
- Plain language (e.g., "We're on track for $2.5M ARR by end of year")
- Optional: one forward-looking metric (hiring plan, forecast)

**Failure modes:**
- Board-level detail (detailed P&L, unit economics by cohort, cap table implications)
- Rosy spin on bad news ("burn is up but we're confident")
- Missing context (number with no trend, forecast, or framing)

---

## Slide 7, Hiring / New Faces

**Purpose:** Welcome new team members and show growth momentum.

**Required elements:**
- Photos or names of new hires (month or quarter)
- Title + brief intro (e.g., "Sarah Chen, VP Sales, joins from Stripe")
- Open roles or hiring priorities for next month
- Optional: fun intro fact per hire ("loves rock climbing") if it fits culture

**Failure modes:**
- New hires introduced with no warmth or context
- Hiring slide buries the ask (teams still hiring but it's not clear)
- Photos missing or low quality

---

## Slide 8, Team Spotlights

**Purpose:** Celebrate individuals and teams who made a difference this month. Sustain morale.

**Required elements:**
- 2-3 spotlights (person or small team, specific win or quality recognized)
- One-liner on what made them stand out (e.g., "Mike shipped our best release sprint ever; zero downtime.")
- Optional: photo grid, quote from a peer, or moment from the month

**Failure modes:**
- Spotlights feel obligatory or repeat the same people
- No connection to real wins (just "nice person")
- Too many spotlights (diminishes impact)

---

## Slide 9, Values / Culture Moment

**Purpose:** Anchor the team to company values and remind everyone of the culture you're building together.

**Required elements:**
- One company value or cultural behavior highlighted with a concrete story
- A real moment from the month (or recent past) that exemplifies it
- Optional: quote from a team member, principle, or manifesto line

**Failure modes:**
- Generic value language with no human moment
- Story that doesn't match the stated value
- Feeling preachy or detached from daily work

---

## Slide 10, Upcoming Events

**Purpose:** Signal what's next for the team (company off-sites, customer events, product launches, hiring push, etc.).

**Required elements:**
- 2-4 upcoming dates or milestones
- One-liner per event (what, when, why it matters)
- Optional: calendar view, theme, or visual anchor

**Failure modes:**
- Events listed with no context on why the team should care
- Crowded calendar that feels overwhelming
- Missing dates or unclear timing

---

## Slide 11, Priorities for Next Month

**Purpose:** Align the team on what matters most in the next 30 days. Specific, actionable, high-level.

**Required elements:**
- 3-5 company-wide or cross-functional priorities (OKRs, shipping targets, hiring goals, etc.)
- One-liner per priority (the outcome, not the task)
- Ownership (team or person leading, if it helps clarity)
- Optional: success metric or "done" definition

**Failure modes:**
- Priorities that are just "keep doing what you're doing"
- Too many priorities (dilutes focus)
- Priorities that duplicate slide 3 or 4 (ship updates vs. next-month targets)

---

## Slide 12, Learnings / Retrospective

**Purpose:** Balance candor. Share what didn't work or what surprised the team. This is the guardrail against "everything's perfect" energy.

**Required elements:**
- 1-2 learnings or setbacks from the month (e.g., "Product roadmap shift delayed feature X", "Customer churn higher than forecast in segment Y", "Onboarding process had friction")
- Honest one-liner per learning (what happened, why it mattered)
- Optional: what you'll do differently next month

**Failure modes:**
- No learnings on a slide labeled "Retrospective" (deck loses credibility)
- Learnings framed as blame instead of growth
- Learnings buried so deep in the deck the team doesn't see them

---

## Slide 13, Thank Yous / Shout-outs

**Purpose:** Amplify team gratitude and celebrate unsung moments.

**Required elements:**
- 3-5 shout-outs (person or team, specific act or moment)
- One-liner per shout-out (what they did, why it mattered)
- Optional: GIF, reaction emoji (restrained), or visual

**Failure modes:**
- Shout-outs that feel obligatory or generic
- Names without context
- Repeating the same people (or ignoring quiet contributors)

---

## Slide 14, Q&A Kickoff

**Purpose:** Placeholder to signal that live Q&A starts here. Invite questions; do not pack the slide with answers.

**Required elements:**
- Simple text: "Questions?" or "Let's talk" or "What's on your mind?"
- Optional: 2-3 prompt questions to seed thinking (e.g., "What should we prioritize next?", "Where are we fragile?")
- Optional: visual (people raising hands, open space, simple icon)

**Failure modes:**
- Slide packed with FAQ answers (defers live conversation)
- No invitation or prompt (leaves people uncertain if questions are welcome)
- Tone that shuts down discussion ("We'll answer common Q&A only")

---

## Slide 15, Open Floor

**Purpose:** Close the meeting. Signal that the conversation continues and the team owns what comes next.

**Required elements:**
- A closing statement (e.g., "This is our month. Let's build.", "Questions? Let's keep going.", "Thank you.")
- Optional: visual anchor, team photo, or moment from the meeting
- Optional: next meeting date or standup channel

**Failure modes:**
- Abrupt ending with no closure
- Tone that feels final (close to conversation, not open to it)
- Missed chance to signal next steps or ongoing channel for feedback

---

## Hard constraints

- **Slide count**: 14 to 16 slides. The spine is 15; plus or minus 1 slide is acceptable if the content shape demands it.
- **Reading pace**: 45 seconds per slide. An all-hands is a live meeting; the deck supports the speaker, not replaces them.
- **No emoji**. Use inline SVG icons from `deck-builder/references/icon-library.md` instead.
- **Balance of celebration and candor**: An all-hands with only wins signals hiding something. Slides 3, 6, and 12 collectively should create a realistic picture (progress and honesty).
- **Financial transparency appropriate for audience**: Company-wide numbers only. No board-level detail (detailed P&L, cohort unit economics, cap table implications, sensitive runway scenarios).
- **Q&A as placeholder**: Slide 14 primes the conversation. The live meeting owns Q&A, not the deck. Keep questions short and focus on engagement.
