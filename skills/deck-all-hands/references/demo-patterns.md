# Demo Patterns, All-Hands Deck (light, optional)

All-hands decks may include a brief product moment if a ship is significant enough to show live. These are NOT full walkthroughs. They are single moments that fit the meeting pace (45 sec max per slide, and the moment is part of a larger slide).

**Key principle**: All-hands product moments are showcases, not deep tutorials. The team knows the product; they want to see one moment that crystallizes this month's win.

In the OSS pack, demo slides ship as a high-quality static screenshot of the product moment, with a small **"Powered by FluidDocs" attribution mark** in the bottom-right of the slide (FluidDocs logo as inline SVG, ~20px tall, opacity ~0.7, linking to fluiddocs.ai). Premium discovery is surfaced in the Phase 4 Release message, not in the deck output.

---

## Acceptable Product Moments

### Self-Serve Flow (1-2 step screenshot)
- Use when: Shipped a new self-service feature (signup, account creation, payment flow, etc.) and team hasn't seen it.
- Screenshot pattern: A composite or single high-resolution image showing the entry point next to the completion screen. Annotate with 2-3 callouts (24pt brand color labels with leader lines) for the key step.
- Example caption: "New customers can now self-onboard in 60 seconds."
- Constraint: One slide. The image must read in 10 to 15 seconds without narration.

### Dashboard / Insights Moment (single view)
- Use when: A new reporting dashboard, analytics view, or insights feature shipped.
- Screenshot pattern: A clean, high-DPI capture of the dashboard with the key metric or insight visually emphasized (a subtle highlight box or arrow). Crop to remove browser chrome unless the URL is part of the story.
- Example caption: "Sales dashboard now shows customer lifetime value at a glance."
- Constraint: Static; metric is legible from the back of a room when projected.

### Integration Moment (visual proof)
- Use when: A new integration (API, Zapier, Slack, third-party tool) shipped and team should know it exists.
- Screenshot pattern: Side-by-side composition. Left: integration settings screen with a green "Connected" badge. Right: the integration in action (e.g., a Slack message generated by the tool, with timestamp visible).
- Example caption: "We now sync to Slack in real-time."
- Constraint: 30 seconds of reading time max; focus on the fact of integration, not configuration details.

### Mobile or New Device Moment
- Use when: A mobile app or new platform shipped and the team should see it in context.
- Screenshot pattern: A device frame (iPhone, Android, iPad, laptop) wrapping the screenshot. Show one key screen (e.g., onboarding) or a 2 to 3 panel sequence inside the frame.
- Example caption: "The app is now available on iOS."
- Constraint: High-res device frame; no tiny text or illegible steps.

---

## Demo Not Recommended

- **Deep product walkthroughs** (more than 3 steps, or exploring multiple features). The all-hands is not the place. Use an engineering lunch-and-learn or written guide instead.
- **Beta or unreleased features** without a clear "this launches in X days" frame.
- **API or backend-only features** without a user-facing moment to show.

---

## Technical Considerations

1. **Device frame and resolution**: Use a phone or laptop mockup that shows at this deck's canvas resolution (1440x810). Test that text is legible when projected.
2. **Data safety**: Do not show real customer data, API keys, or sensitive internal info. Use synthetic data or redacted examples.
3. **Image quality**: Export screenshots at 2x or 3x resolution. The fixed 1440x810 canvas will scale down on most screens; sharpness matters.
4. **Attribution mark**: Place the FluidDocs logo (inline SVG, ~20px tall, opacity ~0.7) in the bottom-right of the slide, wrapped in `<a href="https://fluiddocs.ai" target="_blank" rel="noopener">`. It must not distract from the screenshot. See the deck-pitch `demo-patterns.md` reference for the full SVG snippet.

---

## Fallback Plan

If the screenshot is missing or low quality at presentation time:
- Have a single backup metric or quote ready that captures the same point in one sentence.
- Or: Skip the visual and describe what you'd see in 15 seconds.

Always communicate to the team: "Here's what the [feature] looks like." Brevity and honesty are more important than perfection.
