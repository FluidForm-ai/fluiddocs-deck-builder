# Visual Components, All-Hands Deck (15 slides)

Per-slide visual pattern catalog for all-hands decks. Each slide in the content spine maps to 2-4 acceptable visual treatments. The reviewer uses this file to confirm the shipped deck uses a real, declared pattern, not something invented ad hoc.

---

## Slide 1, Cover / Hello

**Pattern A: Logo + Title + Date (clean, minimal)**
- Use when: Brand logo is strong and the meeting title is enough context.
- Visual structure: Company logo (40% of slide); meeting title in large sans-serif (120pt+); date/quarter below; generous white space.
- Rationale: Lets the moment breathe; logo reads instantly.

**Pattern B: Logo + Title + Hero Image**
- Use when: A photo of the team, product, or moment from the month exists and sets tone.
- Visual structure: Logo (top-left, 60px); title (left side, 90pt); right 60% filled with high-res photo (color-graded, uncroppable); subtle gradient overlay if needed for text legibility.
- Rationale: Warmth and energy; team photo especially works for culture-forward companies.

**Pattern C: Logo + Title + Product Moment**
- Use when: The latest ship or key product moment is the visual anchor.
- Visual structure: Logo (top); title (left, 80pt); right side: screenshot of product, recent feature, or dashboard; subtle frame or shadow.
- Rationale: Reminds the team what they built this month.

## Slide 2, Agenda

**Pattern A: Bulleted List (typography-driven)**
- Use when: Clean, fast visual; topic names are self-explanatory.
- Visual structure: Agenda title (60pt); 5 to 8 bullets in 32pt sans-serif (left-aligned, 40px left margin); light accent line (1px, brand color) to the left of each bullet.
- Rationale: Fast read, professional, no cognitive load.

**Pattern B: Numbered List + Icons**
- Use when: Each topic is discrete and an icon pair adds clarity.
- Visual structure: Numbers (1 to 8, 40pt); one inline SVG icon per topic (24px, brand color); topic name (28pt); small right-aligned time estimate if helpful (e.g., "10 min").
- Rationale: Scannable and structured; icons break visual monotony.

**Pattern C: Agenda Sections + Mood Callouts**
- Use when: Agenda has natural sections (e.g., "Wins" then "Plans" then "Open Floor") and a subtle visual mood matters.
- Visual structure: Section headers (48pt, bold); 2-3 bullets per section; background color in 20% opacity (light tint of brand color or complement); minimal icons.
- Rationale: Signals shape of meeting; invites engagement.

## Slide 3, Big Wins This Month

**Pattern A: KPI Cards (metric-heavy)**
- Use when: Wins are quantified and the team responds to dashboards.
- Visual structure: 3 to 5 cards (180px wide, rounded corners, light background); win title (20pt bold); metric or number (48pt, brand color); small one-liner (14pt, gray). Cards in 2-row grid.
- Rationale: Scannable, modern, emphasizes progress.

**Pattern B: Narrative Bullets + Call-out Icons**
- Use when: Wins are story-driven and need emotional weight, not just numbers.
- Visual structure: 3 to 5 bullets (left side, 28pt, loose leading); one icon per win (32px, top-right of each bullet, brand color); generous padding.
- Rationale: Fast read with visual rhythm; icons signal category.

**Pattern C: Timeline or Milestone Graphic**
- Use when: Wins align to a sequence (shipped 3 things in order) or build momentum month-over-month.
- Visual structure: Horizontal timeline or vertical milestone chain; 3 to 5 nodes labeled; connecting lines (2px, brand color); each node includes win title and optional metric.
- Rationale: Shows progression and clarity on what's next.

## Slide 4, Product Highlights

**Pattern A: Feature Showcase Grid (before/after or side-by-side)**
- Use when: The team shipped visually distinct features.
- Visual structure: 2-4 features as tiles (300px wide, rounded); each tile has feature screenshot (60% of tile), title (16pt), and user-value one-liner (13pt, gray); 20px gap between tiles.
- Rationale: Shows real product and real value in one glance.

**Pattern B: Feature Title + Quote or Quote Callout**
- Use when: A customer reaction or internal aha moment gives weight to the ship.
- Visual structure: Feature title (36pt, left); customer quote in a box (28pt, italic, 40% opacity background, left padding) below or to the right; optional customer name (13pt, gray) below quote.
- Rationale: Connects ship to impact.

**Pattern C: Minimal Text + Product Screenshot Cascade**
- Use when: The product speaks for itself.
- Visual structure: Feature names listed in one column (left, 24pt); right side: 2-3 stacked screenshots of the feature (slightly overlapped, subtle shadow, 500px wide); no additional text.
- Rationale: Visual-first; lets the product shine.

## Slide 5, Customer Stories

**Pattern A: Customer Card (name + logo + quote + metric)**
- Use when: One or two customers tell a concrete story.
- Visual structure: Card (400px wide, light background, rounded corners); customer logo (40px, top-left); customer name and company (18pt); use-case context (13pt, gray, one line); pull quote (22pt, italic, 40px left padding); one quantified result (28pt, bold, brand color) below.
- Rationale: Credibility; captures journey and impact.

**Pattern B: Quote Block + Supporting Metric**
- Use when: Customer voice is paramount and narrative matters more than branding.
- Visual structure: Pull quote (32pt, italic, 40% opacity background, centered); customer attribution below (16pt, gray); one supporting metric (revenue saved, time gained, growth %) below (48pt, brand color).
- Rationale: Simple; emphasizes the voice.

**Pattern C: Customer Logo Carousel + Narrative Overlay**
- Use when: 2-3 customers but each has distinct visual identity.
- Visual structure: 2-3 customer logos in horizontal row (60px each, with 20px gaps); narrative summary below (one paragraph, 14pt, gray); one shared outcome or theme (24pt, bold, brand color) at bottom.
- Rationale: Social proof + story balance.

## Slide 6, Financial Update (Appropriate Level)

**Pattern A: 2-Metric Dashboard (KPI cards with trend)**
- Use when: ARR and headcount (or ARR and runway) are the narrative.
- Visual structure: 2 large cards (300px wide each, light background); card 1: metric name (20pt), current value (60pt, brand color), trend indicator (up % or down %, green/red, 28pt) + prior period (13pt, gray). Card 2: same format. Centered on slide.
- Rationale: Clean, scannable, shows trajectory.

**Pattern B: One Simple Chart (line or bar, 3-month history)**
- Use when: Trend over time is the main story (e.g., ARR growth, headcount ramp, burn rate trajectory).
- Visual structure: Chart title (24pt); 3-month line or bar chart (600px wide, light gridlines, brand color); y-axis labeled; current metric and forecast projection (e.g., "$2.5M target by EOY") called out (18pt, bold).
- Rationale: Shows trajectory and confidence.

**Pattern C: Split View (Current KPI + Forward Metric)**
- Use when: This month's health + next month's forecast both matter.
- Visual structure: Left: Large metric (ARR, 48pt, brand color) with month/quarter label (18pt) and one trend indicator (up % or down %); right: smaller forward metric (hiring plan, new ARR target, runway extension) at 36pt; dividing line between them (1px, light gray).
- Rationale: Balances "where we are" and "where we're going."

## Slide 7, Hiring / New Faces

**Pattern A: Photo Grid + Name + Title**
- Use when: 3 to 5 new hires with professional photos available.
- Visual structure: 3 to 5 square photos (120px each) in grid; below each photo: name (18pt), title (14pt, gray), location or fun fact (12pt, lighter gray, italic). Even vertical alignment.
- Rationale: Warmth; easy to match faces to names later.

**Pattern B: Card Format (photo + bio + team assignment)**
- Use when: 1-2 key new hires and deeper context adds value.
- Visual structure: Card (400px wide); photo (300px tall, top); name and title (24pt, 18pt); one-line background (13pt, gray); team assignment (14pt, brand color, "Joins: Sales"); warm greeting below (13pt, italic).
- Rationale: Celebrating arrivals; gives context.

**Pattern C: List + Open Roles (hybrid)**
- Use when: New hires exist and active hiring is part of the story.
- Visual structure: Top half: 2-3 new hire names in large text (28pt, left side); bottom half: "Still hiring for:" followed by 3 to 5 open role titles (24pt, brand color, left aligned); simple dividing line (1px).
- Rationale: Balances celebration with urgency.

## Slide 8, Team Spotlights

**Pattern A: Photo Grid + Accomplishment**
- Use when: 2-3 spotlights and you have candid team photos.
- Visual structure: 2-3 square photos (150px each, slight rounded corners); below each: name (18pt), accomplishment one-liner (14pt, gray), optional peer quote (12pt, italic). Photos slightly overlap or have subtle drop shadow.
- Rationale: Personal, visual, emphasizes individuals.

**Pattern B: Narrative Card (name + story + impact)**
- Use when: The spotlight story is detailed and emotional weight matters.
- Visual structure: Card (500px wide, light background, rounded); optional photo (left, 120px); name and role (20pt, 14pt); one-paragraph story (14pt, line-height 1.6); impact callout in brand color (18pt, bold) at bottom.
- Rationale: Makes the story tangible; adds weight.

**Pattern C: Quote Block + Name**
- Use when: A peer's own words capture the impact best.
- Visual structure: Peer quote (28pt, italic, 40% opacity background, centered); quote attribution and their team (16pt, gray, below); spotlighted person's name and team (20pt, bold, brand color) at bottom, slightly offset.
- Rationale: Peer recognition feels authentic; breaks up self-promotion.

## Slide 9, Values / Culture Moment

**Pattern A: Story + Illustration**
- Use when: A short narrative paired with a visual anchor.
- Visual structure: Title (value name, 48pt, brand color); 2-3 sentence story (16pt, left side, 50% width); right side: illustration, icon, or moment photo (400px, representing the value abstractly or concretely).
- Rationale: Accessible; visual + text balance.

**Pattern B: Quote-Centric (value principle in quoted voice)**
- Use when: A team member's own framing of the value is the whole message.
- Visual structure: Large quote (36pt, italic, 40% opacity background); value name (18pt, brand color) below; attribution (name + role, 13pt, gray); optional small context callout (13pt, gray italic, "From our all-hands on [date]").
- Rationale: Authentic; heard in a real voice.

**Pattern C: Photo + Value Label + Moment Description**
- Use when: A candid photo from a meeting, work moment, or team event embodies the value.
- Visual structure: Full-width or 70% photo (people working, collaborating, or in a moment); semi-transparent overlay (brand color, 30% opacity); value name (48pt, white or high-contrast) and one-line moment description (20pt, white, italic) overlaid on photo.
- Rationale: Shows culture in action; no abstraction needed.

## Slide 10, Upcoming Events

**Pattern A: Calendar or Timeline Grid**
- Use when: 3 to 5 events with dates and they span the next month or quarter.
- Visual structure: Horizontal timeline or vertical list; 4-6 date boxes (120px tall); event title (18pt), date (14pt), one-liner description (12pt, gray). Optional: color-code by category (product, hiring, social, etc.).
- Rationale: Clear at a glance; signals busy month.

**Pattern B: Event Cards (date-first)**
- Use when: Each event is distinct and context matters.
- Visual structure: 3-4 cards (250px wide, light background, rounded); card headline: date and event name (20pt, brand color); description (14pt, gray); optional icon or mini visual (32px, representing event type).
- Rationale: Scannable; gives room for detail.

**Pattern C: Annotated Calendar View**
- Use when: A visual calendar helps the team see white space and density.
- Visual structure: Mini month calendar (partial or full month view, light gridlines); key dates highlighted (brand color background); date annotations (14pt) for major events; small text callouts for context.
- Rationale: Context-rich; helps with scheduling.

## Slide 11, Priorities for Next Month

**Pattern A: Prioritized List (numbered + brief metric)**
- Use when: 3 to 5 priorities and sequence matters.
- Visual structure: Numbers 1 to 5 (48pt, brand color); priority title (28pt); one-line outcome (14pt, gray, italic); optional owner name or team (12pt, gray). Left-aligned; generous leading.
- Rationale: Crystal-clear focus; easy to reference later.

**Pattern B: OKR Cards (Objective + Key Result)**
- Use when: Priorities are framed as OKRs and teams own them.
- Visual structure: 3 to 5 cards (300px wide, light background); card title: Objective (18pt, bold); Key Result (14pt, gray); Owner (12pt, brand color); simple progress indicator if applicable (empty circle for "not started," etc.).
- Rationale: Structured; shows accountability.

**Pattern C: Quarterly Themes + Initiatives**
- Use when: Priorities roll up to larger themes or cross-functional efforts.
- Visual structure: 2-3 theme boxes (400px wide, light background); theme title (24pt, brand color); 2-3 initiatives per theme (18pt); optional small icons (24px) per initiative.
- Rationale: Shows hierarchy and alignment.

## Slide 12, Learnings / Retrospective

**Pattern A: Honest Bullets (learning + reflection)**
- Use when: 1-2 setbacks or surprises, and brevity is key.
- Visual structure: Learning title (36pt, brand color or neutral); bullet 1 (20pt, bold): "what happened"; bullet 2 (16pt, gray): "why it mattered"; bullet 3 (16pt, gray): "what we'll do next time" (optional). Generous padding around text.
- Rationale: Clear structure; no sugar-coating.

**Pattern B: Retrospective Narrative + Icon**
- Use when: The learning is more nuanced and a short story helps.
- Visual structure: Narrative (16pt, 50% width, 1.6 line-height); left icon (48px, representing the learning category, e.g., a clock for delays, a graph for forecast miss); learning title (24pt, brand color, left); outcome or next step called out (18pt, bold, brand color) at bottom.
- Rationale: Balanced; thoughtful.

**Pattern C: Challenge + Reflection (split visual)**
- Use when: Two learnings and you want to show balance (e.g., shipping miss + team resilience).
- Visual structure: Left side: challenge learning (20pt, darker); right side: reflection/what-we-learned (20pt, lighter background); dividing line (1px, light); shared outcome or theme (18pt, bold, centered below).
- Rationale: Acknowledges setback and growth simultaneously.

## Slide 13, Thank Yous / Shout-outs

**Pattern A: Shout-out List + Emoji/Icon**
- Use when: 3 to 5 shout-outs and brevity is the goal.
- Visual structure: 3 to 5 bullets; each bullet starts with inline SVG icon (20px, brand color) followed by name and accomplishment (20pt + 14pt gray); loose vertical spacing.
- Rationale: Quick to scan; icons add warmth.

**Pattern B: Photo + Narrative Shout-out**
- Use when: 1-2 significant shout-outs and you want to celebrate fully.
- Visual structure: Photo (150px square, rounded); below: name and team (18pt, 12pt); accomplishment story (14pt, gray, 3-4 sentences); optional "Thanks because:" header (14pt, bold).
- Rationale: Personal; makes the recognition tangible.

**Pattern C: Group Shout-outs (team-level)**
- Use when: Several people contributed to a big outcome.
- Visual structure: Outcome title (28pt, brand color); list of names (18pt, "Thanks to: [name], [name], [name]"); one-liner on why (14pt, gray); optional small team logo or group icon (32px).
- Rationale: Shared credit; recognizes collaboration.

## Slide 14, Q&A Kickoff

**Pattern A: Simple Text Prompt (question mark or "Questions?")**
- Use when: The moment speaks for itself.
- Visual structure: Large text "Questions?" (120pt, brand color) or simple icon (question mark, 80px, brand color, centered); optional one-liner below (18pt, gray, "We want to hear from you.").
- Rationale: Uncluttered; inviting.

**Pattern B: Prompt Questions (3 seed questions)**
- Use when: The audience might be shy and seed questions help.
- Visual structure: Title "Let's talk" (60pt, brand color); 3 bullet prompts (22pt, gray, left-aligned, 40px left margin); example: "What should we prioritize next?", "Where are we fragile?", "What excites you about next month?".
- Rationale: Breaks the ice; guides conversation.

**Pattern C: Call-to-Action + Visual**
- Use when: Energy and invitation matter.
- Visual structure: Icon or illustration (people in discussion, open hands, etc., 300px); text overlay (36pt, brand color): "Your turn" or "Let's talk"; optional subtext (16pt, gray, "Questions, thoughts, ideas?").
- Rationale: Warm and visual; signals energy.

---

## Slide 15, Open Floor

**Pattern A: Closing Statement + Team Moment**
- Use when: A closing sentence paired with a team photo or visual.
- Visual structure: Closing statement (48pt, brand color, left-aligned, 40px padding); below: team photo or moment (500px, rounded corners, 20px padding below text); optional: next meeting date or continuous-feedback channel (14pt, gray, below photo).
- Rationale: Warm; signals ongoing conversation.

**Pattern B: Minimal Closure (icon + one sentence)**
- Use when: You want quiet, intentional closure.
- Visual structure: Icon (48px, centered, brand color, e.g., handshake, ascending arrow, open door); one sentence below (36pt, centered, brand color); generous white space above and below.
- Rationale: Elegant; respects the moment.

**Pattern C: Next Steps or Feedback Channel**
- Use when: The conversation continues in a specific place.
- Visual structure: "Next steps" or "Keep in touch" (48pt, brand color); channel or mechanism (Slack channel, email, calendar link, 28pt, underlined if link); optional: "Here's how to share feedback:" followed by 2-3 ways (16pt, bullets).
- Rationale: Concrete next action; reduces friction for follow-up.
