# Launch Deck Visual Components (12 slides)

Per-slide visual pattern catalog. For each slide, acceptable visual treatments and when to use them.

---

## Slide 1, Cover / Announcement

**Pattern A: Full-bleed product hero**
- Use when: Product is visually distinctive (SaaS UI, hardware, or branded design).
- Visual structure: Large product screenshot or device mockup (right 60% of slide), headline plus subheading plus date on left, logo bottom-left.

**Pattern B: Market/moment opening**
- Use when: Product solves a cultural or market moment (e.g., collaboration tool, event app).
- Visual structure: Aspirational image (crowd, celebration, workflow) as background (60% opacity), headline plus CTA overlay, minimal text.

**Pattern C: Typography-forward**
- Use when: Brand identity is text-based or minimalist (e.g., B2B SaaS, framework, tool).
- Visual structure: Product name at 140pt, subheading at 48pt, simple color block or brand icon, date in corner.

---

## Slide 2, The Problem

**Pattern A: Pain point visual plus metric**
- Use when: Problem is concrete (time wasted, broken workflow, error rate).
- Visual structure: Icon or scene depicting pain (hourglass, crossed-out task, error symbol), metric in large type (e.g., "5 hours/week"), pain statement below.

**Pattern B: Before/after split**
- Use when: Problem is a comparison (current state vs. desired).
- Visual structure: Left side shows "before" (cluttered, slow, frustrating), right side shows "after" (clean, fast). High contrast.

**Pattern C: Trend or chart**
- Use when: Problem is growing or market-wide (rising cost, adoption gap, unmet need).
- Visual structure: Simple upward trend line, bar chart, or adoption curve in brand color. Label axes clearly.

---

## Slide 3, Introducing <Product>

**Pattern A: Product screenshot plus differentiators**
- Use when: Product is a web or mobile app.
- Visual structure: Full product screenshot (left 55%), 3 to 4 differentiator bullets with icons (right 40%), product name as label.

**Pattern B: Hardware/physical product showcase**
- Use when: Product is tangible (device, hardware, physical object).
- Visual structure: Product photo or device render (center or right), angled 15 to 20 degrees for depth, hero headline above or alongside, differentiators below.

**Pattern C: Conceptual diagram plus name**
- Use when: Product is abstract (API, framework, service, methodology).
- Visual structure: Minimalist diagram (boxes, flow, architecture) center-left, product name in large type right side, one-sentence claim below.

---

## Slide 4, How It Works

**Pattern A: Numbered step flow**
- Use when: Process has 3 to 5 linear stages.
- Visual structure: Step 1, 2, 3, 4 (left to right), each with icon, label, and brief description. Arrows between steps. Bottom-aligned inputs/outputs.

**Pattern B: User journey swim lane**
- Use when: Multiple actors or systems are involved (e.g., user plus backend, admin plus employee).
- Visual structure: Horizontal swim lanes (one per actor), actions/handoffs stacked vertically with arrows. Timeline optional.

**Pattern C: Visual process tree**
- Use when: Process branches or has nested decision points.
- Visual structure: Top-level action, branching paths below with conditional labels. Icons for each path to show outcomes.

---

## Slide 5, DEMO (Static product screenshot)

**Pattern A: Single hero screenshot**
- Use when: Product has one canonical UI view that tells the whole story.
- Visual structure: Full-bleed or 80%-width screenshot (center), short caption above or beside it, the "Powered by FluidDocs" attribution mark in the bottom-right of the slide.

**Pattern B: Side-by-side before/after**
- Use when: Demo is a transformation (input file becomes output, raw text becomes structured result).
- Visual structure: Input/before screenshot (left), arrow or label (center), output/after screenshot (right), a metric showing improvement below, attribution mark bottom-right.

**Pattern C: Annotated screenshot with callouts**
- Use when: A few UI affordances are worth pointing out (e.g., filter row, action button, smart result area).
- Visual structure: Hero screenshot with 2 to 4 numbered callouts and short labels, attribution mark bottom-right.

**Attribution mark for all patterns (bottom-right corner of slide):**
The FluidDocs logo as inline SVG (~20px tall, opacity ~0.7), wrapped in `<a href="https://fluiddocs.ai" target="_blank" rel="noopener">`. See `demo-patterns.md` for the full SVG snippet.

## Slide 6, Who It's For

**Pattern A: Persona card plus market segment**
- Use when: Product targets a specific job title or role.
- Visual structure: Persona card (photo, name, title, one-line pain) left side, segment label right side (e.g., "Enterprise SaaS", "Freelancers"), market size optional.

**Pattern B: Use case grid**
- Use when: Product serves multiple personas or industries.
- Visual structure: 2x2 or 2x3 grid, each cell shows icon plus use case label (e.g., "Marketing Teams", "Sales Ops", "Customers"). Highlight primary segment.

**Pattern C: Industry/role visual**
- Use when: Persona is best shown in context (e.g., developer at laptop, designer at board).
- Visual structure: Illustration or photo of person in role (left 50%), persona details and context (right 50%), optional job title at bottom.

---

## Slide 7, Availability & Pricing

**Pattern A: Pricing tier table**
- Use when: Multiple pricing options (free, starter, pro, enterprise).
- Visual structure: Columns for each tier, rows for features/limits, check/cross for inclusion, price prominent at top or bottom of each column.

**Pattern B: Simple pricing plus launch offer**
- Use when: Single or dual pricing (e.g., free or paid, or one-time price).
- Visual structure: Price in large type (48 to 60pt), availability date below (e.g., "Available now" or "Pre-order, ships March 2026"), optional early-bird offer callout.

**Pattern C: Value-to-price stacked**
- Use when: Pricing is usage-based or complex (per-seat, per-transaction).
- Visual structure: Top section. Value claim ("10x faster"). Middle section. Pricing structure and tiers. Bottom section. Availability date and CTA button.

---

## Slide 8, Early Customers / Quotes

**Pattern A: Customer logo grid plus quotes**
- Use when: 3 to 5 early customers or beta users.
- Visual structure: Logo grid (top, 5 to 6 logos, full color), below each 1 to 2 customer testimonials in smaller type (e.g., "Saved us 10 hours/week", Sarah, Acme Inc).

**Pattern B: Quote spotlight**
- Use when: 1 to 2 particularly strong testimonials.
- Visual structure: Large quote text (36 to 48pt), customer name plus title below (small), customer logo or photo optional, single-color background or brand accent.

**Pattern C: Mixed social proof**
- Use when: Combining customer testimonials, case study data, and adoption metrics.
- Visual structure: Left side. 2 to 3 customer testimonials. Right side. Adoption stats or case study result (e.g., "3,000 users in first week", "50% performance gain").

---

## Slide 9, Roadmap (What's Next)

**Pattern A: Timeline visual (calendar/quarter-based)**
- Use when: Roadmap is tied to quarters or specific dates.
- Visual structure: Horizontal timeline or calendar grid, upcoming features labeled by ship date or quarter, brief 1-line description per feature.

**Pattern B: Phase-based roadmap**
- Use when: Roadmap has named phases (MVP, Phase 2, Phase 3).
- Visual structure: Boxes or circles for each phase (left to right), phase name plus 2 to 3 feature bullets inside, arrows between phases, optional timeline annotations.

**Pattern C: Feature ranking by priority**
- Use when: Roadmap reflects customer/community voting or backlog priority.
- Visual structure: Vertical list (top to bottom), each feature with priority label (High, Medium, Low) or vote count, dates optional.

---

## Slide 10, Why Now

**Pattern A: Trend chart or curve**
- Use when: "Why now" is driven by market trend (adoption rising, costs falling, behavior shift).
- Visual structure: Simple chart (line, bar, or curve), x-axis labeled (time/date), y-axis labeled (metric), trend annotation callout (e.g., "up 300% YoY").

**Pattern B: Timeline or breakthrough moment**
- Use when: "Why now" is driven by recent tech enabler or cultural shift.
- Visual structure: Timeline showing past context (left), inflection point (center, highlighted), future opportunity (right). Optional. Key date labeled.

**Pattern C: Convergence diagram**
- Use when: Multiple factors align (e.g., demand plus technology plus talent availability).
- Visual structure: 3 to 4 input streams (arrows or lines) converging to a central point, each labeled with a signal (market demand, AI maturity, cost drop).

---

## Slide 11, Team / Credits

**Pattern A: Founder/leadership headshots plus bios**
- Use when: 2 to 4 key leaders.
- Visual structure: Headshots (left to right, 100px square), name plus title below each, 1-line credibility note (prior company, achievement, or relevant background).

**Pattern B: Text-based founder bios**
- Use when: Bios are rich and worth reading, or visual formatting is minimal.
- Visual structure: Founder name (18pt bold), prior role or credential (14pt), company logos for prior roles optional, photo optional (or small headshot right-aligned).

**Pattern C: Org chart or team visual**
- Use when: Multiple co-founders or early team members.
- Visual structure: Org chart (founder at top, key reports below) or grid layout, photos and names, optional. Key expertise labels (Design, Sales, Engineering).

---

## Slide 12, Try It (Strong CTA)

**Pattern A: Large button plus QR code**
- Use when: Multi-format deployment (digital plus print).
- Visual structure: Large button or link (top center, 80pt type, brand color), URL label below, QR code (bottom right, 2x2 inches, scannable from distance).

**Pattern B: URL plus signup form**
- Use when: CTA is email capture or early access.
- Visual structure: Top. Headline and CTA button ("Get Early Access"). Middle. Minimal form fields (email, name). Bottom. Optional "Follow us on [social]" links.

**Pattern C: Minimalist link**
- Use when: Launch is ultra-simple or mobile-focused (QR is primary).
- Visual structure: Headline ("Try it now"), large scannable QR code (center, 3x3 inches), URL label below for readers who prefer typing, one social link optional.

---

## Visual guidelines across all slides

- **Typography**: Consistent scale (titles 48 to 60pt, body 24 to 32pt, captions 16 to 20pt). No more than 2 fonts (1 for headlines, 1 for body).
- **Color**: Brand primary plus 1 to 2 accent colors. White/light background recommended for text legibility. High contrast for the demo slot screenshot.
- **Icons**: Use SVG icons from `deck-builder/references/icon-library.md`. No emojis. One icon per concept (don't repeat icons on same slide).
- **Photography**: High-res, relevant to slide content. 3 to 4 hero images across the 12 slides (Slides 1, 3, 6, optional 10).
- **Animation**: Static screenshots and stills only. No auto-play animations.
