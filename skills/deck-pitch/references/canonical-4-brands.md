# Canonical 4 Pitch-Deck Brands (cached catalog)

Cached color palettes, typography, logo source notes, screenshot concepts, visual-component affinities, and historical anchors for the 4 canonical pitch-deck templates shipped with the OSS pack. This is a **lookup convenience**, not ground truth, for any Mode A build, re-verify against the brand's own page (see `deck-builder/references/brand-methodology.md` for the research protocol).

Google Fonts stand-ins are given where the real brand font isn't freely available.

When a pitch deck is requested for a brand NOT in this catalog, run the methodology in the core pack. If the user rebuilds that brand often, add it to this catalog after the first deck ships.

---

## 1. Airbnb (canonical reference)

**Palette**
- Rausch `#FF5A5F` (primary)
- Rausch dark `#E0484D`
- Babu `#00A699` (secondary)
- Arches `#FC642D` (accent)
- Hof `#222222` (ink)
- Foggy `#767676` (muted)
- Cement `#DDDDDD` (border)
- Paper `#F7F7F7` (surface)

**Typography**
- Display: Poppins (stand-in for Cereal)
- Body: Inter (stand-in for Cereal)

**Logo**: Belo, the trademarked A-loop heart shape. Use the accurate path.

**Visual component affinity**: Competition equals 2x2 matrix. Moat equals 4-card grid plus flywheel. GTM equals motion grid or city-rollout rail. Market equals TAM rings (with crescent-placement fix). Traction equals growth curve. Team equals 3-founder equal-grid or 3 founders plus key-hires strip.

**Screenshot concept for slide 6**: Browser-frame screenshot of a listing detail page, hero photo, host card, calendar, "Reserve" CTA. Real-looking listing title, location, price per night. No Lorem ipsum.

**Historical grounding anchor** (for a "classic" Airbnb deck circa 2008-2009): Seed-stage, "Book rooms with locals, rather than hotels," 3 co-founders (Brian Chesky, Joe Gebbia, Nathan Blecharczyk), Y Combinator W09, AirBed & Breakfast original framing.

## 2. Stripe

**Palette**
- Indigo `#635BFF` (primary)
- Violet `#7A73FF` for gradient accent
- Ink `#0A2540`
- Paper `#F6F9FC`
- Muted `#425466`
- Success green `#00D924`

**Typography**
- Display: Sohne or Camphor Pro, stand-in **Inter** (with tight tracking)
- Body: Inter
- Mono: Source Code Pro or JetBrains Mono (heavy use, Stripe is API-forward)

**Logo**: The Stripe wordmark in indigo, or the "S" bug.

**Visual component affinity**: Competition equals bold data table. Moat equals depth bars. GTM equals motion grid or channel bars. Market equals bottom-up calc. Traction equals logo grid plus NRR or growth curve. Team equals 3-founder equal-grid or 2-founder hero split.

**Screenshot concept for slide 6**: API-doc aesthetic, browser frame, code editor on the left (cURL, Node, Python tabs), response JSON on the right. Real endpoint, real keys redacted, real charge object. Or a mini dashboard screenshot: MRR chart, recent payments list, subscription lifecycle. Pick one.

## 3. Anthropic

**Palette**
- Tan / Coral `#D4A27F` or `#CC785C` (primary accent, Claude brand)
- Cream `#F5F1EB` (surface, Claude cream)
- Ink `#191919`
- Paper `#FAF7F2`
- Muted `#787068`

**Typography**
- Display: Styrene B or Copernicus, stand-ins **Inter** (sans) plus **Fraunces** or **Source Serif 4** (literary serif for pull quotes)
- Body: Inter

**Logo**: The Anthropic "A-star" mark (8-pointed orange burst) plus wordmark.

**Visual component affinity**: Competition equals narrative card stack or feature bars. Moat equals capabilities ladder. GTM equals wedge narrative. Market equals single big number plus credibility. Traction equals quiet-confidence metrics. Team equals narrative team intro.

**Screenshot concept for slide 6**: Browser-frame screenshot of a Claude-style chat with a long thinking or artifact panel. Tool use indicators, citations, literary prose feel. Real prompt, real response excerpt, no Lorem ipsum.

## 4. Sequoia Classic

**Palette**
- Black `#000000` (primary)
- White `#FFFFFF` (surface)
- Accent red `#B91C1C` (used sparingly for emphasis)
- Warm grey `#57534E`, `#A8A29E`, `#E7E5E4`

**Typography**
- Display: Tiempos, stand-in **Fraunces** (variable serif)
- Body: Inter or GT America, stand-in **Inter**

**Logo**: Sequoia wordmark in the serif (Tiempos). Minimal.

**Visual component affinity**: Competition equals narrative card stack. Moat equals memo-style ("what happens when a giant copies this"). GTM equals wedge narrative. Market equals single big number plus credibility. Traction equals quiet-confidence metrics. Team equals narrative team intro.

**Screenshot concept for slide 6**: Less product-driven, this is a memo-style template. The "demo" slide can be a screenshot of a live investment-memo reading view with expandable sections. Or a portfolio-company scoreboard. Frame in a clean browser chrome, or no chrome at all (editorial full-bleed).
