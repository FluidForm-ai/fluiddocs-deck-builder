# Visual Components · Sales Deck Patterns (11 slides)

Per-slide visual pattern catalog for sales decks. Each slide entry lists 2-4 acceptable visual treatments with rationale. Use these patterns to ensure shipped decks employ real, declared patterns, not invented ad hoc.

---

### Slide 1 · Cover

**Pattern A: Typography-Centered (Client Focus)**
- Use when: professional B2B context; emphasizing the partnership
- Visual structure: client logo top-left (40-60px), meeting context centered (36pt+ headline, e.g., "[Company], Sales Engagement"), date/time bottom-right (small, 14pt), optional confidentiality corner. White space generous (10% margin all sides).
- Component count: 4-5 text elements (logo, headline, context line, date, confidentiality if present). No unnecessary graphical elements.
- Typography: logo 40-60px, headline 36-48pt, context 24pt, date 14pt
- Color: brand primary accent for headline, neutral for date/context
- Rationale: Clean, professional, establishes this is account-specific (not a template). Logo balance signals partnership.

**Pattern B: Split-Screen Problem/Promise**
- Use when: the pain-solution metaphor is visually clear (chaos to calm, fragmented to unified)
- Visual structure: left 50% is "before" mood (warm colors, cluttered or stressed visual), right 50% is "after" mood (brand primary, clean/organized). Subtle vertical divider line in center.
- Component count: 2 visual zones, 1-2 text anchors per zone (4-5 words max). Logo positioned top-left or top-right outside the split.
- Typography: zone labels 18-24pt, minimal text (avoid paragraphs)
- Color: left zone uses warning/warm palette (orange, coral), right zone uses brand primary plus clean neutrals
- Rationale: Visual metaphor is memorable; the divide communicates transformation without words.

**Pattern C: Data-Anchored Context**
- Use when: one key industry stat or company metric sets the meeting tone
- Visual structure: client logo (top-left, 40px), large metric or stat (48-56pt, e.g., "78% of [vertical] report X challenge"), headline below (32pt), date/time footer. Metric is the visual anchor.
- Component count: 5-6 elements (logo, stat, headline, context, date). Stat should dominate the canvas.
- Typography: stat 48-56pt (bold), headline 32pt, supporting text 14-18pt
- Color: stat in brand primary, headline in neutral, supporting text in 60% gray
- Rationale: Stat creates credibility hook; prospect sees "they know my industry" immediately.

### Slide 2 · About You (Discovery Summary)

**Pattern A: Timeline Growth Story**
- Use when: prospect's growth trajectory is the hook (recent funding, hiring spree, product launches, market expansion)
- Visual structure: vertical or horizontal timeline (bottom or left), 4-5 milestone events (dates plus one-liner descriptions), prospect logo anchored at top/left, visual accent (mini icons or color dots) per milestone. Milestones should span 12-24 months.
- Component count: 4-6 milestone cards, timeline spine, logo, 1-2 context labels. Total: 8-10 visual elements.
- Typography: milestone date 14pt, description 14-16pt, logo 40px, timeline axis 10-12pt
- Color: milestones alternate brand primary (odd) and 70% gray (even); timeline spine in light gray; logo full color
- Rationale: Time progression grounds the narrative; visual shows you've tracked their growth.

**Pattern B: 2x2 Market Position Grid**
- Use when: mapping their competitive or organizational position (e.g., "Company Stage" vs. "GTM Complexity", or "Revenue Scale" vs. "Data Maturity")
- Visual structure: 2x2 quadrant grid, axis labels clear (10-14pt), prospect positioned as a dot or small icon in one quadrant. Axes should represent two dimensions relevant to your solution.
- Component count: 4 quadrants, 2 axes, 1 prospect marker, 2 axis labels, optional legend. Total: 7-8 elements.
- Typography: axis labels 12-14pt, quadrant labels 10pt (if used), prospect quadrant label 14-16pt bold
- Color: prospect quadrant in 20% brand primary tint; other quadrants in 10% neutral gray; axes in light gray; prospect marker in brand primary
- Rationale: Spatial positioning is intuitive; 2x2 is industry-standard. Shows you understand their market position.

**Pattern C: Key Facts Sidebar**
- Use when: key stats are the evidence (headcount, ARR range, recent hiring volume, market cap)
- Visual structure: left vertical sidebar (30% width, brand primary background), 4-6 facts stacked (each with an icon from the icon library, label plus metric). Right 70% reserved for a visual (org structure, revenue line, screenshot of their product). Sidebar and right area should have visual balance (neither should dominate).
- Component count: 4-6 fact cards (icon plus label plus metric each), 1 major visual on right, 1 logo anchor. Total: 12-14 elements.
- Typography: sidebar header 16pt white, each fact label 12pt white, each metric 18-24pt white, right-side labels 14-16pt neutral
- Color: sidebar brand primary, icons white or light brand, text white on sidebar. Right-side visual in natural color plus neutral grays.
- Rationale: Sidebar concentrates key data; right visual provides context. Icon breaks up text.

**Pattern D: Org Context plus Team Spotlight**
- Use when: their org structure or specific team changes are the insight (new hire announcement, team expansion, reporting line change)
- Visual structure: simplified org chart showing 2-3 levels relevant to your sale (e.g., CFO, Controller, Accounting Manager), or a team-roster callout (e.g., "Data team: 8 people, hiring 3 senior engineers"). Highlight the team most affected by your solution in brand primary box or color.
- Component count: 4-8 org boxes, 2-3 connection lines, 1-2 hiring callouts, 1 context label. Total: 8-12 elements.
- Typography: titles 14-16pt, names 12pt, callout metrics 18pt bold
- Color: affected team in brand primary background, other teams in neutral gray, connection lines in light gray, hiring callouts in warm accent
- Rationale: Org chart shows decision makers and affected teams; builds credibility that you understand the structure.

### Slide 3 · The Problem (Their Pain, Their Words)

**Pattern A: Large Quote plus Supporting Metric**
- Use when: you have a direct quote from a discovery call that encapsulates the pain
- Visual structure: large, left-aligned quote in serif font (28-36pt, italic, 1-2 lines max), attribution below (name, title, company, 12-14pt, neutral gray), one supporting metric or small chart to the right (e.g., trend showing problem escalation, error count, time waste in hours). Quote should dominate the left 60% of the slide.
- Component count: 1 quote, 1 attribution, 1 metric/chart, optional: 1 icon next to quote. Total: 3-4 elements.
- Typography: quote 28-36pt (italic), attribution 12-14pt, metric 24-32pt bold, chart axis labels 10pt
- Color: quote in dark gray or brand primary, attribution in 70% gray, metric in brand primary, chart uses neutral plus 1 warning color for problem zone
- Rationale: Direct voice is credible; quote captures emotion; metric provides rigor.

**Pattern B: Before/After Workflow Split**
- Use when: the problem has a clear outcome (manual to automated, fragmented to unified, slow to fast)
- Visual structure: left 45% labeled "Today" (shows messy workflow, screenshot of manual process, or cluttered UI), right 45% shows the impact visually (e.g., chart of wasted hours, error count, revenue leakage). Center 10% has a divider or arrow showing the progression.
- Component count: 2 visual zones, 2 zone labels, 1 divider/arrow, 2-3 supporting metrics. Total: 6-8 elements.
- Typography: zone labels 20-24pt bold, metrics 18-24pt, supporting text 14pt
- Color: "Today" zone in warm/warning palette (light orange, coral, or red tint background), impact zone in bold brand primary or warning red, divider in neutral
- Rationale: Side-by-side visual tells the story without words; colors communicate emotion.

**Pattern C: Stacked Problem Hierarchy**
- Use when: they have one big problem with 3-4 supporting sub-problems or dimensions
- Visual structure: large headline problem at top (32-40pt), 3-4 supporting sub-problem boxes stacked below (24-28pt headlines), each sub-box has an icon (from icon library: time icon, cost icon, quality icon, risk icon) and 1-2 line description (14pt). Boxes are colored neutrally with icon fill in brand primary.
- Component count: 1 headline, 4 sub-boxes (icon plus label plus description each), optional top-level metric. Total: 12-15 elements.
- Typography: headline 32-40pt bold, box headlines 24-28pt, descriptions 14-16pt, metrics 20pt
- Color: headline neutral dark, boxes light gray background, icons brand primary, description text dark gray
- Rationale: Hierarchy shows one overarching problem; icon-coding helps scanning.

**Pattern D: Enormous Single Metric**
- Use when: ONE quantified problem tells the entire story (e.g., "15 hours/week wasted", "1.3M data errors annually", "$2.5M revenue leakage")
- Visual structure: metric displayed at 120-180pt bold, centered or left-aligned, 1-2 line explanation below in 20pt (plain English describing what the metric means), optional: small supporting stat in 16pt below that. Metric should occupy 50%+ of the visible slide area.
- Component count: 1 large metric, 1-2 explanatory lines, optional supporting context. Total: 2-3 elements.
- Typography: metric 120-180pt bold, explanation 20-24pt, context 16pt
- Color: metric in brand primary or warm accent (if it's a risk metric, use warning orange/red), explanation in neutral dark gray
- Rationale: Single dominant number is memorable; explanation grounds it in reality.

### Slide 4 · Why Today (Cost of Inaction, Urgency)

**Pattern A: Timeline with Consequence Gap**
- Use when: urgency is tied to a specific business milestone (hiring deadline, compliance date, product launch, budget cycle closing)
- Visual structure: horizontal timeline (bottom 40% of slide), "today" marked clearly (vertical line or marker), consequence date marked 3-12 months out. Gap between them is shaded in warning color (light orange or red). Top 60% shows the impact of the gap (lost revenue, missed hiring targets, compliance risk, etc. in 18-24pt bold).
- Component count: 1 timeline spine, 2 date markers, 1 gap zone, 1-2 impact callouts. Total: 5-6 elements.
- Typography: dates 12-14pt, impact callout 20-28pt bold, context 14-16pt
- Color: timeline neutral gray, "today" marker in dark neutral, consequence zone in warning orange/red, gap shaded light warning (10% opacity)
- Rationale: Visual timeline is intuitive; shaded gap creates urgency without feeling manipulative.

**Pattern B: Loss Calculator Cards (3-Part)**
- Use when: you can quantify the cost of inaction across 3 dimensions (weekly cost, total cost by deadline, opportunity cost)
- Visual structure: 3 cards in a row (left-to-right), each with a label (20pt), a large number (40-48pt bold), and a subtext (12-14pt). Cards alternate light neutral background and brand primary tint. Numbers in card 1 and 3 are in warm/warning, card 2 in brand primary or neutral to show cumulative impact.
- Component count: 3 cards, each with 3 text elements (label plus number plus context). Total: 9 elements.
- Typography: card label 16-20pt, number 40-48pt bold, context 12-14pt
- Color: card 1 and 3 numbers in warning red/orange, card 2 in brand primary; card backgrounds alternate neutral (white/light gray) and 10% brand primary tint
- Rationale: Three cards parallel the three dimensions of loss; numbers dominate; card structure is scannable.

**Pattern C: Trend Projection Chart**
- Use when: the problem is getting worse and future trajectory is clear (error rate climbing, team turnover climbing, customer churn accelerating)
- Visual structure: line chart showing historical trend (12-24 months, solid line in neutral gray), projected future (dashed line in warning color, extending 6-12 months out), "today" marker (vertical dashed line), consequence zone shaded (light warning color). X-axis shows months/quarters, Y-axis shows the metric (error count, hours wasted, $ at risk, etc.). Legend shows historical vs. projection.
- Component count: 1 line chart, 1 historical series, 1 projection series, 1 marker, 1-2 callout boxes pointing to consequence zone. Total: 6-8 elements.
- Typography: axis labels 10-12pt, legend 10pt, callout text 14-16pt, metric labels 12pt
- Color: historical trend neutral gray (or subtle brand color), projection in warning (orange/red, dashed), consequence zone light warning tint (10% opacity), marker line dark neutral
- Rationale: Chart shows deterioration; dashed projection line signals "if nothing changes"; consequence callout creates urgency.

**Pattern D: Paired Obstacles Card Layout**
- Use when: urgency has two independent drivers (e.g., "market is moving fast" plus "you're constrained by legacy systems"; or "hiring window closing" plus "competitor just raised funding")
- Visual structure: two side-by-side cards (left: "Reason to Act Now", right: "Window of Opportunity"), each card has a headline (24pt), 2-3 supporting bullets (14pt), and a color theme. Left card warmer (problem), right card brand primary (opportunity). Cards should be equal width (~45% each).
- Component count: 2 cards, each with headline plus 3 bullets plus optional icon. Total: 8-10 elements.
- Typography: card headline 24-28pt bold, bullets 14-16pt, metric callouts 18-20pt
- Color: left card background light warm (10% orange tint), text dark gray. Right card background light brand (10% brand primary tint), text dark gray or brand primary.
- Rationale: Two-card layout balances problem and opportunity; equal real estate signals both are important.

### Slide 5 · Our Solution (Plain-Language)

**Pattern A: Headline plus Capability Pyramid**
- Use when: your solution's value stacks logically (capability 1 enables 2, which enables 3)
- Visual structure: "We [verb] so you [outcome]" headline (32-40pt, top-center), below that a 3-tier pyramid or stacked boxes (capability 1 at bottom, 2 in middle, 3 at top). Each tier is labeled (20pt) plus one supporting detail (14pt). Right side (40% width) is a product screenshot or use-case diagram showing the capability in action. Pyramid background is light (10% brand tint); right-side visual in full color or natural product UI.
- Component count: 1 headline, 3 capability boxes, 3 labels, 3 details, 1 product visual. Total: 11-12 elements.
- Typography: headline 32-40pt bold, tier labels 20-24pt bold, details 14-16pt, visual context 12pt
- Color: headline brand primary or neutral dark, pyramid tiers alternate light neutral and 10% brand tint, tier labels dark gray, right-side visual in natural product colors
- Rationale: Pyramid shows progression; product visual provides credibility that it's real.

**Pattern B: Capability Stack with Arrows**
- Use when: the workflow is sequential (data input, processing, intelligent output)
- Visual structure: 3-4 vertical or horizontal stacked boxes, each labeled (20pt), with downward/rightward arrows between them. Bottom box labeled "Data Input" (or equivalent), middle boxes show processing stages, top box "Outcome" or "Intelligence". Each box is 40-60px tall, 200-250px wide. Left side (20% width) is context text (your headline plus 2 supporting bullets, 14pt each). Right side (20% width) is optional: one supporting stat or icon.
- Component count: 4 boxes, 3 arrows, 1 context panel, 1-2 icons, optional metric. Total: 10-12 elements.
- Typography: box labels 16-20pt bold, context text 14-16pt, arrows 12pt label text
- Color: boxes alternate light neutral and 10% brand primary tint, top box "outcome" is brand primary with white text, arrows light gray or brand primary
- Rationale: Stacked boxes are clear; arrows show causality; outcome box has visual weight (darker or brand color).

**Pattern C: Value Promise Card (Emotional or Cultural)**
- Use when: the benefit is about how they work, not what they do (teams move faster, engineers ship features not plumbing, less firefighting)
- Visual structure: large central value statement (2-3 words, 100-140pt bold, brand primary, centered), surrounded by 4-6 supporting details (smaller type, 14-16pt, neutral gray, positioned around the central statement in cardinal/diagonal directions). Small logo badge top-left corner (brand primary background, white text, 10-12pt). Generous white space around central statement.
- Component count: 1 central value statement, 4-6 supporting details, 1 logo badge, optional: 1-2 supporting icons. Total: 6-10 elements.
- Typography: central statement 100-140pt bold, supporting details 14-16pt, logo badge 10pt
- Color: central statement brand primary, supporting text neutral gray, logo badge white on brand primary, background white
- Rationale: Large statement is memorable; surrounding details anchor it; white space signals elegance.

**Pattern D: Before/After Capability Comparison**
- Use when: showing the shift in how they work (manual to automated, siloed to integrated, reactive to proactive)
- Visual structure: two columns (left 45%, right 45%), center 10% divider with arrow or divider line. Left column labeled "Without [Product]" (20pt), shows manual/broken state via screenshot, workflow diagram, or metaphor (messy desk, fragmented sticky notes, etc.). Right column labeled "With [Your Product]" (20pt), shows automated/clean state. Column backgrounds: left uses warning/neutral grays, right uses brand primary and clean palette.
- Component count: 2 column zones, 2 labels, 2 visuals, 1 divider/arrow. Total: 6-8 elements.
- Typography: column labels 20-24pt bold, supporting text 14-16pt in each column
- Color: left column neutral/gray background, right column brand primary or clean background. Text contrast maintained (dark text on light background, light text on dark if needed).
- Rationale: Before/after is immediately understood; colors communicate the shift emotionally.

### Slide 6 · How It Works (Product Mechanics)

**Pattern A: Numbered Step Sequence with Icons**
- Use when: the workflow is linear and each step is distinct (Step 1, Step 2, Step 3, Step 4)
- Visual structure: 4-5 steps displayed left-to-right in a row, each step shows: (1) a number circle (brand primary background, white number, 24-28pt diameter), (2) a product screenshot or icon (40-60px), (3) a one-line label below (14-16pt). Steps connected by right-pointing arrows (light gray, 2-3pt). Each step background is light neutral or subtle brand tint.
- Component count: 4-5 step boxes, 3-4 arrows between them, 4-5 icons/screenshots, 4-5 labels. Total: 14-18 elements.
- Typography: step number 24-28pt bold white, label 14-16pt dark gray, step context 12pt gray (optional)
- Color: number circles brand primary, step backgrounds alternate white and 5% brand tint, arrows light gray or brand primary, icons/screenshots in natural colors
- Rationale: Number sequence is familiar; icons provide visual variety; arrow flow is intuitive.

**Pattern B: Static Screenshot Sequence**
- Use when: the customer needs to see the actual UI to believe it; OSS decks ship with static-only screenshots (working interactive demos are a hosted feature)
- Visual structure: 3-4 static screenshots of the UI in sequence, with subtle numbered overlays (1, 2, 3) and one-line captions below each (14pt). Sequence should show: input, processing, output. Optional: one success-outcome callout next to the final screenshot (e.g., "Reconciliation complete: 95% automatic match").
- Component count: 3-4 screenshots, 3-4 captions, optional success-outcome callout. Total: 6-10 elements.
- Typography: screenshot labels 12-14pt, caption 14pt, callout 16-18pt bold, outcome 18-20pt
- Color: screenshots use product's actual UI colors (not re-skinned). Callouts and annotations in brand primary or neutral accent.
- Rationale: Static screenshots always render; product's real colors maintain authenticity; sequence shows progression without requiring live demo infrastructure.

**Pattern C: System Integration Diagram (Simplified)**
- Use when: the process involves multiple systems or teams (their data, your platform, their system plus BI tool)
- Visual structure: 4-5 boxes arranged left-to-right: "Data Input" (their system or data source), "Your Platform" (center, brand primary box), "Output Destinations" (their system or reporting tool). Arrows show data flow with directional labels (e.g., "pulls data hourly", "writes clean data"). External boxes (their systems) in neutral gray, your platform in brand primary. Minimal internal detail (no "database calls Kafka", etc.).
- Component count: 4-5 system boxes, 3-4 data flow arrows, 4-5 arrow labels, optional: 1-2 legend items. Total: 12-14 elements.
- Typography: box labels 16-20pt bold, arrow labels 12-14pt, legend 10pt
- Color: their systems neutral gray (or company color if you know it), your platform brand primary, data flow arrows light gray or brand primary, text dark gray or white depending on background
- Rationale: Simple diagram shows integration without overwhelming; labeled arrows clarify data direction.

**Pattern D: Flowchart with Decision Points**
- Use when: the process has optionality or branches (e.g., "automatic match if greater than 95% confidence, else manual review")
- Visual structure: boxes for steps, diamond shapes for decision points, clear paths (happy path in bold or bright color, exception paths in lighter color). Typical layout: 5-8 boxes/diamonds total, arranged in a top-to-bottom or left-to-right flow. Happy path (expected flow) highlighted or in brand primary; exception paths in neutral or light gray.
- Component count: 5-8 nodes (boxes plus diamonds), 5-7 edge paths, 1-2 legend items for path types. Total: 13-16 elements.
- Typography: node labels 12-14pt, path labels 10-12pt, legend 10pt
- Color: happy path boxes/edges brand primary or bright (60% opacity), exception path neutral gray or light warning, decision diamonds light blue or neutral
- Rationale: Decision branching shows complexity honestly; color coding guides the eye to the happy path.

### Slide 7 · Proof (Named Customers, Specific Outcomes)

**Pattern A: Logo Grid plus Detail Card**
- Use when: you have 2-4 customer logos in similar verticals
- Visual structure: top 50% shows 2-4 customer logos in a row or 2x2 grid (each logo 60-100px, grayscale or muted tones, light background box around each). Bottom 50% shows one detailed proof card: customer name (24pt bold), vertical plus size (16pt gray), challenge (2 bullets, 14pt), solution (2 bullets, 14pt), results (3-5 metrics in 18-20pt bold, brand primary), optional quote (14pt italic). Card has brand primary left border (3-5px).
- Component count: 2-4 logos, 1 detail card header, 4-8 detail card bullets, 3-5 metrics. Total: 14-20 elements.
- Typography: logo context 12-14pt (optional), detail name 24pt bold, detail section headers 16-18pt, detail bullets 14pt, metrics 18-20pt bold
- Color: logos grayscale on white boxes, detail card white background with brand primary left border, metrics brand primary, quote dark gray italic
- Rationale: Logo row builds confidence quickly; detail card proves the outcomes are real.

**Pattern B: Case Study Deep-Dive (One Large)**
- Use when: you have one really strong reference in their exact vertical
- Visual structure: top-left quadrant (30% of slide) has customer logo (60-80px), customer name (20pt bold), title/role (14pt gray). Top-right (30%) has challenge statement (headline 18-20pt bold, 2-3 bullets below 12-14pt). Bottom-left (30%) shows solution details (headline 18-20pt bold, 2-3 bullets 12-14pt). Bottom-right (30%) shows quantified results: 3-5 large metrics (28-36pt bold, brand primary), each with a label (14pt gray). Optional: customer photo or product screenshot bottom-center.
- Component count: 1 logo, 1 name, 4-section layout (challenge, solution, results each have headline plus bullets), 3-5 metrics. Total: 18-24 elements.
- Typography: logo context 14pt, name 20pt bold, section headlines 18-20pt bold, bullets 12-14pt, metrics 28-36pt bold
- Color: logo in full color, name/role dark gray, challenge section neutral background (5% tint), solution section 5% brand tint, results section metrics in brand primary
- Rationale: Quadrant layout is organized; metrics dominate the visual; spatial separation clarifies each dimension.

**Pattern C: Testimonial plus Metric Stack**
- Use when: a customer quote is the strongest evidence
- Visual structure: top 50% is a large quote (28-36pt, serif, italic, centered or left-aligned, 2-3 lines max), attribution below quote (name, title, company logo, 14-16pt, gray). Bottom 50% shows 3-4 quantified results in card format: each card has a large metric (32-40pt bold), a label below (14pt gray), optional icon (from icon library). Cards are arranged horizontally or in a grid, light neutral background, brand primary metric text.
- Component count: 1 quote, 1 attribution with logo, 3-4 metric cards (metric plus label plus optional icon each). Total: 12-16 elements.
- Typography: quote 28-36pt italic serif, attribution 14-16pt gray, metrics 32-40pt bold, labels 14pt gray
- Color: quote dark gray, attribution light gray, metric cards light neutral background (white or 2% tint), metrics brand primary, icons brand primary
- Rationale: Quote is credible and memorable; metric stack quantifies the voice claim.

**Pattern D: Vertical-Specific Proof Gallery**
- Use when: you have multiple customers in their exact vertical (e.g., 3-4 healthcare customers, or 3-4 SaaS fintech customers)
- Visual structure: left 40% displays 3-4 customer logos stacked vertically (each 60-80px, with company name below, 14pt). Right 60% aligned with each logo is a one-line result in 18-20pt (e.g., "reduced billing errors by 65%", "cut claims processing from 14 to 5 days"). Bottom, spanning full width, is a shared outcome stat (32-40pt bold, brand primary, e.g., "Average cost savings: $850K annually").
- Component count: 3-4 logos, 3-4 names, 3-4 one-line results, 1 shared outcome. Total: 11-16 elements.
- Typography: logo context 14pt gray, result one-liners 18-20pt bold dark gray, shared stat 32-40pt bold brand primary
- Color: logos grayscale or muted color, company names dark gray, one-liners neutral dark gray, shared stat brand primary, background white with subtle bottom border highlight for shared stat
- Rationale: Vertical stack of logos is easy to scan; parallel one-liners show consistent results; shared stat is the payoff.

### Slide 8 · ROI / Value (Numbers, Not Claims)

**Pattern A: Waterfall Chart (Input, Savings, Net ROI)**
- Use when: the math is the primary story; showing how you get from costs to savings to payback
- Visual structure: 4-5 boxes arranged left-to-right or in a cascade, each showing a value with a label: "Annual Cost" (orange box, $X), "Implementation" (orange box, $X), "Annual Savings" (green box, $Y), "Year 1 Net Value" (green box, $Y minus $X), "Payback Period" (blue box, "X months"). Connecting lines or arrows show progression. Total cost zone on left (warm colors), savings zone on right (cool/green colors).
- Component count: 4-5 value boxes, 3-4 connecting arrows/lines, 1 legend. Total: 8-12 elements.
- Typography: box label 12-14pt, value 24-32pt bold, payback 14-16pt
- Color: costs warm (light orange), savings green or brand primary, net result bold, payback blue or neutral, text white on colored boxes, dark on light boxes
- Rationale: Waterfall visualization shows the complete math; color progression (warm to cool) is intuitive.

**Pattern B: Simple Calculation Table**
- Use when: ROI depends on 3-4 key variables that are transparent
- Visual structure: 4-column table (Variable | Assumption | Impact per unit | Total), 3-4 rows (e.g., "Users" | "20" | "$X savings per user" | "$Y total"), totals/summary row at bottom in bold. Header row brand primary background (white text), data rows alternate white and 2% gray, totals row bold with 5% brand tint background.
- Component count: 1 table (4 columns x 4-5 rows), optional: 1 legend or note below. Total: 20-24 table cells plus optional note.
- Typography: header 14pt white bold, data 12-14pt dark gray, totals 14pt bold dark gray, note 10pt gray
- Color: header brand primary background, data rows white/light gray alternate, totals row 5% brand tint background, text dark gray or white for contrast
- Rationale: Table format is precise and scannable; alternating rows aid readability; totals row is obvious.

**Pattern C: Side-by-Side Scenario Cards**
- Use when: ROI varies significantly by deployment size or usage, and you want to show typical deployments
- Visual structure: 2-3 cards side-by-side (equal width), each card labeled with a scenario ("Small Deployment: 10 users", "Standard: 50 users", "Large: 100+ users"). Each card shows: annual cost (20pt bold, neutral), annual savings (24pt bold, green or brand primary), payback period (16pt, gray), one recommended callout if applicable (e.g., "most customers"). Cards have light neutral background, subtle border.
- Component count: 2-3 scenario cards, each with 4-5 data points. Total: 8-17 elements depending on card count.
- Typography: scenario label 18-20pt bold, cost 16-18pt neutral, savings 24-28pt bold, payback 14-16pt, recommended callout 12pt italic
- Color: card backgrounds light neutral (white or 2% gray), cost text neutral gray, savings text green or brand primary (bold), recommended callout in soft warning highlight (e.g., light yellow tint)
- Rationale: Scenario cards let prospect "find themselves" in the numbers; visual differentiation makes the common case obvious.

**Pattern D: Payback Period Timeline**
- Use when: payback period is the key story (when do they break even, and what does ROI look like after that?)
- Visual structure: horizontal timeline bar (2-3 inches long, center), divided into two zones: pre-breakeven (light warning, 10% opacity) and post-breakeven (brand primary, 10% opacity). Breakeven point marked with a vertical line and label ("6 months"). To the left of breakeven, cost/investment is shown (label plus $X). To the right, annual ROI is shown (label plus "4:1" or "$4 value per $1 spend"). Optional: mini markers for quarterly milestones.
- Component count: 1 timeline bar, 2 zone fills, 1 breakeven marker, 1-2 value labels, optional 4 quarterly markers. Total: 7-10 elements.
- Typography: breakeven point 16-18pt bold, cost label 14pt, ROI label 14pt, quarterly markers 10pt
- Color: pre-breakeven light warning (10% opacity), post-breakeven brand primary (10% opacity), breakeven marker dark neutral line, labels dark gray
- Rationale: Timeline bar is intuitive; color contrast shows the shift; post-breakeven ROI ratio answers "how much value after they recoup cost?"

### Slide 9 · Implementation (Timeline, Effort, Onboarding)

**Pattern A: Gantt-Style Timeline (Phases plus Duration plus Owners)**
- Use when: the timeline spans 8-16 weeks with multiple phases
- Visual structure: Gantt chart with 5 phases listed on left (rows: Discovery, Setup, Training, Go-Live, Optimization), timeline bars on right showing duration (weeks labeled), milestone markers (small diamond or flag icons) at key dates, owner assignment shown within or below each bar (e.g., "YourCo, Weeks 1-2" in brand primary, "Client, Weeks 2-3" in neutral). X-axis shows weeks (0-16+) with month/quarter markers.

- Color: your team phases brand primary bar, their team phases neutral gray bar, milestone markers dark neutral or brand primary, text white on colored bars, dark gray on neutral bars
- Rationale: Gantt is industry-standard for project timelines; color-coding ownership is immediate; milestone markers show critical junctures.

**Pattern C: Milestone Cards (Sequence View)**

**Pattern D: Phase Overview plus Responsibility Grid**

### Slide 10 · Pricing (Clear, No Hand-Waving)

**Pattern A: Price Card(s) with Inclusions**

- Rationale: Large price card makes pricing unavoidable; inclusions show what they get; CTA signals next action.

**Pattern B: Price plus ROI Alignment Card**

**Pattern C: Tiered Pricing Table**

- Color: formula dark gray, scenario cards white background with light brand tint border, scenario total brand primary bold, recommended scenario has light yellow or accent background tint
- Rationale: Formula is transparent and clear; scenario cards let prospect calculate their own cost; highlighting typical size guides expectation.
