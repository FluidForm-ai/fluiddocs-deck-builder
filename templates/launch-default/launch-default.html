04 · How it works
Four steps, one canvas

From kickoff to retro
without leaving Atlas.

Atlas captures the work as it happens. No status decks, no parallel trackers, no end-of-week sync to reconcile a hundred edits.

01

Frame the launch

Pick a template or start blank. Atlas pre-fills the spine, the milestones, and the owners from your last comparable launch.

02

Wire the teams

Connect Slack, Linear, Notion, Salesforce, and Gmail in a click. Atlas tracks work in the tools your team already uses.

03

Ship in public

Status auto-rolls from the work itself. A single shareable URL replaces the recurring 30-minute status meeting.

04

Compound the playbook

Every launch becomes a template. The team gets faster, the documents stay current, the institutional knowledge stops walking out the door.

06 · Who it serves
Built for operators

Two kinds of teams,
one canvas.

Primary · Heads of launch
The operating
lead at a Series B.
Owns 6 to 10 launches a year. Reports up to a CEO, sideways to product, marketing, and sales. Lives in three planning tools and answers status questions all day.
  • Runs cross-functional rollouts without a project manager
  • Needs a single artifact every stakeholder will actually open
  • Wants the post-mortem written from real data, not memory
Secondary · Founding teams
The founder
who is the PMM.
Two to twenty people. Every launch is the most important one. No budget for a launch tool, no patience for one that takes a week to set up.
  • Wants a starter playbook in the box
  • Templates that don't need a workshop to fill in
  • A free tier that survives the first three launches
